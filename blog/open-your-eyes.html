Open Your Eyes

May 15, 2024 7 min read Thoughts

How many more thousands of children have to die?

How many more families need to be torn apart?

How long will the world stay silent?

How long will you keep your eyes closed?

Did you sleep well last night? Did you enjoy your breakfast this morning? How was your day yesterday? And tomorrow? I'm sure you have plans, don't you?

As you go about your daily routine, taking comfort in the familiar, there are others who can't foresee the next hour, let alone the next day. Their nights are restless, their mornings uncertain.

While we look forward to future joys and celebrations, they cling to fleeting moments of peace. Take a moment to reflect — what if your everyday was replaced with their reality? The world is vast, and our experiences varied; Do not forget the stories that remain untold, the lives waiting for a chance at normalcy!

Listen closely, you'll hear their silent cries...

The heartbeats of innocent children, once filled with the promise of a vibrant future, are being extinguished every day. In places where the laughter of a child should echo, there is instead a haunting, terrifying silence. The playgrounds that once resonated with joy are now, simply, scarred.

Families are being torn asunder. The bonds that should remain unbreakable are tested beyond limits. Parents, faced with the unbearable task of burying their young, are left with voids that can never be filled. Siblings, instead of sharing stories of adventure, are sharing shelters, wondering if they'll be lucky enough to be together at all!

The places they called home are reduced to rubble; the memories of better times buried beneath the debris. Their hopes and aspirations, like the very ground they walk on, are clouded by the dust of destruction.

To believe that these stories are too distant, too separate from our reality, we silence the voices that need to be heard the most. Let their stories resonate, let their cries for help find an echo in our hearts. Because every life, no matter where it's lived, holds immeasurable value.

Image: Humanitarian crisis in Palestine

The Power of Awareness

It's easy to scroll past the headlines, to dismiss the statistics as mere numbers. But behind each number is a person – with dreams, fears, and a life as rich and complex as your own. When we choose to engage with these realities, to truly see them, we take the first step towards meaningful change.

Awareness is the precursor to action. By opening our eyes to the suffering of others, we allow ourselves the opportunity to help, to speak out, to make even the smallest difference. It begins with acknowledgment – a conscious decision to not turn away from the uncomfortable truths that exist alongside our comfortable lives.

"The world will not be destroyed by those who do evil, but by those who watch them without doing anything." - Albert Einstein

From Awareness to Action

Being aware is just the beginning. True compassion calls us to action. While the scale of suffering may seem overwhelming, remember that even small actions, when multiplied, create significant impact:

  • Educate yourself: Seek out reliable sources of information about ongoing humanitarian crises.
  • Share responsibly: Use your voice and platforms to spread awareness, always fact-checking before sharing.
  • Support humanitarian organizations: Even small donations can make a difference when combined with others.
  • Advocate: Contact elected representatives and demand action on humanitarian issues.
  • Stay engaged: Maintain your attention and care even when the news cycle has moved on.

The greatest disservice we can do is to allow ourselves to become desensitized. To view human suffering as inevitable, as someone else's problem. In a world more connected than ever before, we cannot claim ignorance. We must choose to see, to feel, to act.

A Call for Empathy

Empathy is not merely feeling sorry for those in difficult situations; it's about genuinely trying to understand their experience. It's about recognizing our shared humanity, despite geographical, cultural, or ideological differences.

When we open our eyes to the struggles of others, we also open our hearts. We begin to see the world not through the narrow lens of our personal experience, but through the expansive vision of collective human experience.

This broader vision enables us to act with more compassion, to make decisions that benefit not just ourselves but others as well. It pushes us to question systems and structures that perpetuate suffering, and to imagine and work toward alternatives.
